Hyrox Training Plan: Bulk (March - August) & Prep (August - November)

This plan is designed to build muscle and strength from March to August, then transition into Hyrox-specific endurance, conditioning, and race efficiency from August to November. It balances strength, hypertrophy, and functional fitness to optimize performance for Hyrox.

March - August: Strength & Size Focus (Bulking Phase)

During this phase, the focus is on increasing muscle size and strength while maintaining a base level of conditioning for Hyrox. Running is included once per week to maintain endurance without interfering with muscle growth. The training split consists of five days, incorporating compound lifts, accessory work, and functional movements.

Training Split (5 Days)

Day Focus
Day 1 Push (Bench-Focused)
Day 2 Pull (Deadlift-Focused)
Day 3 Legs (Squat-Focused)
Day 4 Full Body Strength & Power
Day 5 Hyrox Conditioning + Run

Strength Progression (Main Lifts)

The main lifts (bench press, deadlift, squat, overhead press, weighted pull-ups) follow a wave progression to maximize strength while avoiding burnout. The goal is to progressively overload by increasing weight every 2-3 weeks while maintaining form.

Weeks Sets x Reps Intensity
1-4 4x5 75-80% 1RM
5-8 4x4 80-85% 1RM
9-12 3x3 85-90% 1RM
13-16 5x5 70-75% (Deload)

Hyrox Conditioning Progression (Day 5)

Hyrox-specific movements are included once a week to ensure continued skill development. This session focuses on sled pushes, SkiErg, rowing, wall balls, and running.

Weeks SkiErg Row Sled Push Wall Balls Run
1-4 300m x3 300m x3 20m x3 15 reps x3 400m finisher
5-8 400m x3 400m x3 25m x3 20 reps x3 800m finisher
9-12 500m x3 500m x3 30m x3 25 reps x3 1K finisher
13-16 500m x4 500m x4 30m x4 30 reps x3 1.5K finisher

August - November: Hyrox Prep Phase

During this phase, training shifts towards Hyrox-specific endurance, transitions, and efficiency. Strength work is maintained but with reduced volume, while running and high-intensity work increase.

Training Split (5 Days)

Day Focus
Day 1 Strength & Power (Lower Body)
Day 2 Hyrox Conditioning (Intervals & Functional Work)
Day 3 Strength & Power (Upper Body)
Day 4 Hybrid Endurance & Hyrox Skills
Day 5 Long Run or Hyrox Simulation

Running Progression (2-3x per week)

Running volume increases to improve endurance. The plan alternates between steady-state runs and interval work to develop race pace and overall capacity.

Weeks Steady Run Intervals
1-4 6-8K 6 x 400m
5-8 8-10K 8 x 400m
9-12 10-12K 6 x 800m

Full Hyrox Simulation (Every 3-4 Weeks)

As race day approaches, full Hyrox simulations should be completed to practice pacing and transitions.

Exercise Distance/Reps
Run 1K
SkiErg 1000m
Sled Push 50m
Sled Pull 50m
Burpee Broad Jumps 80m
Rowing 1000m
Farmers Carry 200m
Wall Balls 100 reps

Final 4 Weeks (Peak & Taper)

The final four weeks focus on optimizing performance, reducing fatigue, and ensuring peak conditioning.

Weeks Focus
9-10 Increase race-specific training, full simulations
11-12 Taper (Reduce volume, 1-2 light runs, mobility work)
