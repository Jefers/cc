Before You Start

Make sure your webcam is connected and you have about 15-20 minutes to complete all the steps.

Progress 0%
1
Check Internet Speed

Go to speedtest.net. Click "Go" and write down your results.

What to look for:

  • Upload speed should be at least 3-5 Mbps for smooth streaming
  • If it's under 2 Mbps, the internet is the problem

Fix: Ask others in the house to stop using Wi-Fi, or plug the PC directly into the router with a cable.

2
Check Webcam Settings

Open the Logitech Camera App (Logi Capture or G HUB) and check these settings:

What to look for:

  • Resolution: Set to 720p or 1080p, not lower
  • Frame Rate: 30 fps is good
  • Focus: Turn off auto-focus if the picture keeps going blurry
  • Exposure/Lighting: Try turning off auto-exposure if the image is too bright or dark

Fix: Save your settings and close other camera apps ‚Äî only your streaming app should be using the webcam.

3
Check PC Performance

Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc to open Task Manager. Look at CPU, Memory (RAM), and GPU usage.

What to look for:

  • If CPU or Memory is near 90-100%, your PC is struggling
  • If you see many background apps, right-click and choose "End task" (except for system ones)

Fix: Close extra tabs and apps (Chrome, games, etc.). Restart your computer before streaming.

4
Test Camera on Another App

Open the Camera app in Windows. Check if the picture looks clear there.

What to look for:

  • ‚úÖ If it looks clear ‚Üí problem is with the streaming software
  • ‚ùå If it looks blurry ‚Üí problem is with the webcam or lighting

Fix: Clean the lens with a soft cloth. Add a light in front of you (not behind).

5
Check Streaming Software Settings

Open your streaming app (e.g., OBS, browser-based stream) and check Video Settings.

What to look for:

  • Resolution: 720p (start here)
  • Bitrate: 2500-4000 kbps
  • Frame rate: 30 fps

Fix: If the stream lags or blurs, lower resolution (from 1080p to 720p). If it's pixelated, increase bitrate (if your internet allows).

6
Reboot Everything

Restart your devices to fix "mystery problems."

This often fixes issues that don't have an obvious cause.

7
Test Everything Together

Do a short private test stream (most platforms allow this). Watch the playback.
