Pro Setup for Full-Length Self Portraits

iPhone 16 Pro Max • Professional Photography Guide
1

Use the Rear (Main) Camera

The 1x main lens (24mm equivalent) gives the best quality and least distortion.

⚠️ Avoid the 0.5x ultra-wide — it will stretch proportions.

2

Tripod Placement

Set the tripod so the camera lens is roughly at your mid-torso or chest height, not eye level.

→ This prevents the "big head/small feet" distortion and keeps proportions natural.

Ensure the phone is perfectly vertical (use the built-in level guide).

3

Distance and Framing

Stand about 10–15 ft (3–4.5 m) from the camera for a natural perspective.

Use the 3x lens if space allows — it compresses perspective slightly for flattering body proportions.

4

Camera Settings

Open Camera → Photo mode → 1x or 3x lens (avoid Portrait mode unless you want background blur).

Tap your face or upper body to focus, then slide exposure down slightly to avoid blown highlights.

Turn on Grid in Settings → Camera → Grid (for horizon and body alignment).

5

Lighting

Use soft, even light — near a large window or outdoors on an overcast day.

⚠️ Avoid harsh overhead light; it shortens the body and creates strong shadows.

6

Triggering the Shot

Use your Bluetooth remote or 3–10 sec timer to avoid camera shake.

For natural poses, start moving or adjusting slightly before the shutter — it reduces stiffness.

7

Fine-tuning

Review the photo and check for:

→ Straight verticals (no leaning forward/back).
→ No distortion in legs or head (adjust tripod height slightly if needed).
→ Balanced framing (equal space above head and below feet).
