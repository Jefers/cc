Personal Training Digital Strategy

Key Points

  • Research suggests websites remain vital for businesses, even with high social media usage, especially for detailed information and transactions.
  • It seems likely that social media, including Facebook, can drive significant traffic to websites, benefiting your personal training business.
  • The evidence leans toward using multiple platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook to build brand visibility and direct users to your website for conversions.

Convincing Plan for Your Success

Hey there! After our chat where you said, “people don’t really use websites anymore, young people don’t use Facebook anymore either, it’s all TikTok and Instagram,” I’ve been digging into the data. You’re spot on about social media’s rise, but I think we can blend that with a killer website I’m building for you to supercharge your online personal training gig. Here’s the plan, backed by facts, to get us on the right track—light-hearted, ambitious, and ready to roll!

Why Websites Still Rock

Social media’s huge—over 5 billion users globally (Smart Insights, 2025)—but websites aren’t dead. They’re where people go for the nitty-gritty, like your services, testimonials, and a client hub. A Pew Research survey shows folks still hit websites after spotting a business online, making it perfect for conversions.

Social Media as Your Traffic Booster

Facebook’s got 3.07 billion users, Instagram 2 billion, TikTok 1.58 billion (Backlinko, 2025). They’re goldmines for visibility! Studies like Barqar’s analysis show social media can drive 60% of referral traffic to websites. Personal trainers thrive here—check out Marketing 360’s case study where social posts funneled clients to a site for sign-ups.

The Multi-Platform Play

Let’s hit all angles: Instagram for pics, TikTok for vids, Facebook for ads. Then, point everyone to your website. Data from Sprout Social backs this multi-channel vibe. Here’s how:

Platform Strengths Strategy
Instagram Visual content, young crowd Workout vids, before-afters, link in bio
TikTok Short, viral vids Quick tips, challenges, website link
Facebook Ads, broad reach Targeted ads, success stories, site links

Let’s Make It Happen

I’ll build a mobile-friendly site (60.66% of traffic’s mobile, per Allconnect), with all the goodies—services, hub, offers. We’ll post killer content on social, run some Facebook ads (they’re ace for leads), and track what works with tools like Similarweb. This combo’s our ticket to growing your online training empire—let’s do this!
