Tagalog Learning Course Curriculum

This is a 30-day beginner curriculum for friendly, informal Tagalog/Filipino conversation and text messaging. The goal is not formal business language. The goal is to understand and use everyday phrases with Filipino friends in their twenties, while staying respectful and not sounding forced.

Learning goals

  • Build a daily habit of learning and reviewing useful phrases.
  • Understand basic greetings, reactions, questions, invitations, jokes, and chat replies.
  • Learn phrase chunks before grammar theory.
  • Recognize when a phrase is casual, playful, affectionate, teasing, or too strong.
  • Avoid stiff textbook language unless it is genuinely useful.
  • Maintain a personal phrase bank for situations that come up with real friends.

How the 30-day plan works

  • One small daily topic: 5–10 phrases, a short tone note, and a tiny practice.
  • Spaced review: every seventh day is lighter and reviews previous phrases.
  • Inbox influence: phrases you hear or want to say in inbox.md should shape future lessons.
  • Source of truth: this Markdown file is rendered into pages/curriculum.html.

30-day roadmap

Day Theme Learning outcome Practice idea
1 Warm greetings Say hi, ask how someone is, and reply naturally Send three versions of “how are you?” from neutral to playful
2 Quick chat replies Use short text-message responses like “okay,” “sure,” “wait,” and “really?” Build a mini reply bank for messages
3 Introductions without stiffness Say your name, explain you are learning, and ask for patience Write a casual self-intro
4 Pronunciation survival Learn vowels, stress awareness, and how to avoid sounding robotic Read 10 phrases aloud slowly
5 Asking simple questions Ask “where,” “what,” “who,” “when,” and “why” in casual contexts Turn five English questions into Tagalog chunks
6 Food and eating Handle “have you eaten?”, snacks, cravings, and invites to eat Practice a food chat with a friend
7 Review 1 Consolidate greetings, replies, intro, and food phrases Choose your top 12 phrases and rate confidence
8 Compliments and appreciation Compliment friends naturally without sounding intense Write friendly compliments for outfit, photo, food, and effort
9 Laughing and reactions Use common reactions: funny, cute, shocking, awkward, annoying Make a reaction ladder from mild to strong
10 Making plans Ask if someone is free, suggest hanging out, and confirm Draft a casual invite text
11 Time and availability Talk about today, later, tomorrow, busy, and free Create five “I’m free/busy” messages
12 Directions and places Ask where someone is and say where you are going Practice location check-in texts
13 Feelings and mood Say tired, happy, hungry, sleepy, stressed, excited Make an honest mood update
14 Review 2 Consolidate social planning and reaction phrases Write a 10-message mini chat
15 Friendly teasing Understand light teasing, “joke lang,” and safe playful wording Label phrases as safe, risky, or too much
16 Slang foundations Learn common casual words and abbreviations without overusing them Add tone tags to 10 slang items
17 Texting style Use particles and fillers like “naman,” “lang,” “ba,” and “kasi” as chunks Rewrite stiff messages to sound more casual
18 Caring phrases Check on someone, say take care, and show concern casually Create care messages for sick, tired, and sad friends
19 Apologies and small fixes Say sorry, explain briefly, and smooth awkward moments Draft apologies from light to serious
20 Opinions and preferences Say what you like, do not like, want, and prefer Build a preference list about food/music/plans
21 Review 3 Consolidate slang, texting tone, and caring phrases Record your top 20 phrase bank
22 Photos and social media Comment casually on photos, stories, selfies, and updates Write five story replies
23 Music, games, and hobbies Ask about interests and react to recommendations Make a hobby Q&A script
24 Filipino humor basics Notice exaggeration, playful complaint, and self-deprecating jokes Translate reactions without making them too literal
25 Group chat survival Greet a group, react briefly, and join without dominating Write group chat entrance/exit lines
26 Voice-note / spoken flow Make short spoken messages sound less stiff Record or read a 30-second casual update
27 Boundaries and respect Decline, say no worries, and avoid overfamiliar wording Practice polite but casual declines
28 Review 4 Consolidate real-world chat situations Simulate a full conversation from greeting to goodbye
29 Personal phrase bank Build your reusable phrase list for actual friends Choose 30 phrases you would really use
30 Reset and next month Review progress and design the next 30 days around real conversations Write what situations you want next

Monthly refresh instructions

When the plan is refreshed, it should:

  1. Read inbox.md, progress.md, progress.json, and recent journal entries.
  2. Preserve completed learning history.
  3. Keep practical phrases and texting situations near the front.
  4. Add slang only when tone and risk are explained.
  5. Render pages with python3 scripts/render_lab.py.
